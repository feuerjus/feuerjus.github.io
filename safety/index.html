Aircraft Safety Systems

TCAS, runway overrun protection, GPWS, and more — the technology that makes flying the safest mode of transport.

TCAS — Traffic Collision Avoidance System

TCAS (pronounced "tee-cas") is a mandatory system that helps prevent mid-air collisions by monitoring the airspace around the aircraft using transponder signals.

How It Works

TCAS interrogates nearby transponder-equipped aircraft and tracks their altitude, range, and bearing. It calculates if another aircraft poses a collision risk based on time to closest approach (tau) and altitude separation.

Advisories

  • TA (Traffic Advisory): Alerts the crew that another aircraft is within 20–48 seconds of closest approach. The pilot should visually acquire the traffic and be ready to respond.
  • RA (Resolution Advisory): Issues a specific vertical maneuver command (e.g., "Climb, climb!" or "Descend, descend!") to ensure safe separation. Pilots must follow RAs immediately, even if it contradicts ATC instructions.

TCAS II (version 7.1) is the current standard, with enhanced logic and clear voice annunciations. It coordinates maneuvers between two TCAS-equipped aircraft so they don't both climb or descend in the same direction.

Runway Overrun Prevention

ROPS — Runway Overrun Prevention System

Developed by Airbus, ROPS provides in-cockpit alerts and automatic braking to prevent runway excursions. It continuously monitors the aircraft's position, speed, and deceleration against the remaining runway length. If the system calculates an overrun risk, it alerts the crew and can engage maximum autobrake.

RAAS — Runway Awareness and Advisory System

RAAS (by Honeywell) provides aural advisories to increase crew situational awareness on the ground, such as "Approaching runway 27L," "On runway 27L," and "Long landing" if the aircraft touches down far along the runway.

EMAS — Engineered Materials Arresting System

EMAS is a bed of crushable concrete blocks installed at the end of runways where limited safety area exists. If an aircraft overruns, the EMAS material crushes under the landing gear, decelerating the aircraft safely to a stop.

GPWS & EGPWS — Ground Proximity Warning Systems

GPWS (Ground Proximity Warning System) uses radar altimeters to warn pilots when the aircraft gets too close to terrain. It generates aural warnings such as "Pull up!" and "Terrain, terrain!"

EGPWS (Enhanced GPWS, also known as TAWS — Terrain Awareness and Warning System) adds a worldwide terrain database and a forward-looking terrain prediction function. This gives pilots earlier warnings and a visual display of surrounding terrain, dramatically reducing the risk of controlled flight into terrain (CFIT).

Autoland & Autobrake Systems

Autoland

Modern aircraft can perform fully automatic landings using the Instrument Landing System (ILS) or GBAS. Autoland is certified for Category IIIb conditions (50 ft decision height, 200 m RVR), enabling safe landings in dense fog. Dual or triple autopilot redundancy ensures continued safe operation even if one system fails.

Autobrake

Autobrake applies braking automatically after touchdown to achieve a selected deceleration rate (LOW, MED, MAX). It engages immediately upon landing, reducing pilot workload and ensuring consistent stopping performance. MAX autobrake provides maximum deceleration for short or contaminated runways.
