Airbus A350

The next-generation widebody — engineered for efficiency, range, and passenger comfort.

Overview

The A350 is Airbus's newest widebody airliner, designed to compete with the Boeing 777 and 787. Entering service in 2015 with Qatar Airways, it features a revolutionary composite airframe, advanced aerodynamics, and Rolls-Royce Trent XWB engines — the most efficient large turbofan ever built.

Composite Airframe

Over 50% of the A350's structure is made from composite materials, including carbon-fiber-reinforced polymer. The fuselage is built from four large composite barrel sections, reducing weight and eliminating thousands of aluminum alloy rivets. The wings are also largely composite, contributing to exceptional aerodynamic performance.

This composite dominance results in lower fuel burn, reduced maintenance requirements, and higher cabin humidity (thanks to corrosion resistance) — improving passenger comfort.

Rolls-Royce Trent XWB

The A350 is exclusively powered by the Rolls-Royce Trent XWB, specifically designed for the airframe. Key features:

  • Thrust range: 74,000–97,000 lbf
  • Fan diameter: 118 inches (3.0 m)
  • 17% better fuel burn than previous Trent engines
  • Advanced blisk (bladed disk) technology in the intermediate-pressure compressor
  • Up to 99.9% operational reliability

Variants

  • A350-900: 300–350 seats, 8,100 nmi (15,000 km) range
  • A350-1000: 350–410 seats, 8,700 nmi (16,100 km) range — the longest-range variant
  • A350ULR: Ultra-long-range version of the -900, capable of 9,700 nmi (18,000 km), used by Singapore Airlines for Newark–Singapore, the world's longest flight

Passenger Comfort

The A350 was designed with passenger experience as a priority. It features the widest economy seat in its class (18 inches), larger windows, higher ceilings, and LED mood lighting. The cabin is pressurized to 6,000 feet (rather than the standard 8,000), reducing jet lag and improving passenger well-being on long flights.
