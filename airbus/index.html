Airbus

From a European consortium to a global aerospace leader.

Overview

Airbus SE is a European multinational aerospace corporation founded in 1970. It is one of the world's largest aircraft manufacturers, competing primarily with Boeing. Known for innovation, Airbus introduced the first fly-by-wire airliner and continues to push boundaries with composite materials and advanced aerodynamics.

History

The Early Years — A300 & A310

Airbus launched with the A300, the world's first twin-engine widebody. It proved that a twin-engine could safely operate transatlantic routes (ETOPS), paving the way for modern long-range twins. The shorter A310 followed, offering improved efficiency.

The A320 Family Revolution

Launched in 1988, the A320 introduced digital fly-by-wire (FBW) controls to commercial aviation. Instead of mechanical linkages, the pilot's inputs are interpreted by computers, providing protection envelopes and common type ratings across the family (A318, A319, A320, A321).

Widebody Success — A330 & A340

The A330/A340 family offered airlines a choice of twin-engine efficiency or four-engine capability. The A330 became a long-haul workhorse, while the A340 served routes requiring extended overwater operations before ETOPS liberalization.

The A380 — A New Standard

The A380, the world's largest passenger airliner, entered service in 2007. With 550+ seats, double-deck configuration, and quiet cabins, it represented a bold bet on hub-to-hub travel. Though production ended in 2021, it remains a marvel of engineering.

Modern Era — A350 & A220

Airbus now focuses on the A350 (next-generation widebody) and the A220 (formerly Bombardier C-Series) for the smaller end of the market, alongside the ever-popular A320neo family.

Key Innovations

  • Fly-by-Wire: First introduced on the A320, now standard across all Airbus aircraft.
  • Common Cockpit: Similar cockpit layouts across families reduce pilot training costs.
  • Composite Structures: The A350 uses over 50% composites by weight.
  • Side-stick Controls: Replaces traditional control yokes for a modern cockpit design.
