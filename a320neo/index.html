Airbus A320neo

New Engine Option — quieter, cleaner, and more efficient.

Overview

The A320neo (New Engine Option) is the upgraded version of the best-selling A320 family. Launched in 2010 and entering service in 2016, it delivers 20% better fuel efficiency per seat compared to the original A320, thanks to new engines and aerodynamic improvements.

Engine Options

CFM International LEAP-1A

The LEAP-1A features advanced technology including 3D-printed fuel nozzles, ceramic matrix composite shrouds, and a larger fan diameter. It offers a 15% improvement in fuel consumption over CFM56 engines used on the A320ceo.

Pratt & Whitney PW1100G (GTF)

The PW1100G Geared Turbofan uses a gear system to allow the fan and low-pressure turbine to spin at different optimal speeds. This results in exceptional fuel efficiency and significantly reduced noise. The GTF is one of the quietest engines in commercial service.

Performance & Specifications

  • Range: Up to 3,500 nautical miles (6,500 km) with Sharklets
  • Capacity: 150–240 passengers depending on variant
  • Fuel efficiency: 20% reduction in fuel burn per seat
  • NOx emissions: 50% below CAEP/6 standards
  • Noise: 50% reduction in noise footprint compared to A320ceo

Variants

  • A319neo: 140 seats, 3,750 nmi range
  • A320neo: 180 seats, 3,500 nmi range
  • A321neo: 240 seats, 4,000 nmi range — also serves as basis for A321XLR (ultra-long range)

The A321XLR (Extra Long Range) extends the reach to 4,700 nautical miles, enabling transatlantic routes with a single-aisle aircraft — a game-changer for airline economics.

Cockpit & Commonality

The A320neo retains the same fly-by-wire cockpit as the A320ceo, allowing pilots to transition with minimal additional training. This common type rating extends across the entire A320 family, and even shares similarities with the A330 — a major operational advantage for airlines flying both types.
